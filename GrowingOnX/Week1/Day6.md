# Day 6 — Friday, 8 May 2026

**Theme:** Ship the pinned post. Friday is the highest-engagement day of the week for tech/builder content. This is the most important block of work in your first week.

## Today's actions

### 1. Final polish + visual generation (2 hours, morning)
- Read the full draft out loud once more. Cut another 10% of words.
- Address every `// FIX` and `// BREAK` marker from yesterday
- Generate any diagrams/screenshots needed — gpt-image-2 for stylized visuals, Excalidraw or Figma for technical diagrams
- Verify every tweet ≤ 280 chars
- Paste the full thread into a thread tool (Typefully, Tweet Hunter, or X's native composer) to verify it parses correctly

### 2. Publish at peak window (5 min)
**Best windows for tech/builder X audiences:**
- **9:30am IST** — morning India + late-night SF/PST overlap (often the best window)
- **8:30pm IST** — afternoon US + all-day Europe overlap (second-best)

Pick one and ship. Do not post off-peak.

### 3. Pin the thread (1 min)
Profile → ⋯ menu on the thread → "Pin to your profile."

### 4. First-4-hours engagement blitz (3 hours, contiguous block)
This is the most important block of your week. The X algorithm front-loads engagement signals — what happens in the first 4 hours determines whether the thread gets picked up by recommendations or dies in the timeline.

- Reply to every comment on your thread within 15 min
- Quote-tweet your own thread once with a different angle as the QT text (a one-line reframe that highlights one specific tweet inside the thread)
- Send **20 substantive replies** to watch-list accounts during this window — those replies earn curiosity clicks back to your now-pinned thread
- DM 5 close friends/colleagues asking for an honest read (not a like — a *read*). Specifically ask: "did the argument hold up?"

### 5. End-of-day reflection (15 min)
In `Week1/day-6-publish-log.md`, capture:
- Time of post (HH:MM IST)
- Impressions at +1h, +4h, +24h-projected
- Top-performing single tweet within the thread (which one had the highest individual impressions)
- Any replies that went unexpectedly deep — those are signals for future content
