# Day 7 — Saturday, 9 May 2026

**Theme:** Honest weekly review. The plan adapts to data, not the other way around.

## Today's actions

### 1. Pull the week's numbers (45 min)
In `Week1/week-1-review.md`, capture:
- Followers gained (start vs end)
- Total impressions across all posts
- **Best-performing single post** (impressions, replies, profile clicks, follows-from-this-post)
- **Best-performing single reply** (impressions, replies, profile clicks)
- **Pinned post 24-hour metrics**: impressions, replies, profile-clicks-to-follow conversion rate
- **Best-performing tweet *inside* the pinned thread** (which individual tweet pulled the most impressions)

### 2. Honest assessment (30 min)
Three questions, written answers in `week-1-review.md`. Be specific — vague self-talk produces vague next weeks:

1. **What worked?** Which post format / topic / time / phrasing earned engagement above your baseline? Quote specific posts.
2. **What flopped?** Which post format / topic / time / phrasing underperformed? "My hot takes flopped" is too vague — name the specific post.
3. **What surprised you?** Reactions you didn't predict, either positive or negative.

### 3. Plan Week 2 (60 min)
Open `Week2/plan.md`. Decide:
- **Friday thread topic for Week 2** — book it now, don't wait until Wednesday. Pick from your `pinned-post-options.md` runners-up or from new ideas surfaced this week.
- **5 short post topics** for Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu/Sat (one per weekday)
- **3 watch-list adjustments** — which accounts to drop (didn't engage back / wrong audience overlap), which to add
- **One specific experiment** — try something different in one slot. (Different post time? Different format? A poll? A direct take you've been holding back?)

Then create `Week2/Day1.md` through `Week2/Day7.md` using the same structure as this week's day files.

### 4. Light maintenance (30 min)
- 5 replies on watch list (Saturday is lower-stakes; lighter cadence acceptable)
- 1 short post

### 5. Rest

This is not optional. The Mercury AD window is finite but you'll burn out before it closes if every day is full-tilt. Saturday evening = no X. Sunday = lighter day. The chart wants depth, and depth requires recovery.

## End-of-day check
- [ ] Week 1 numbers logged in `week-1-review.md`

- [ ] `Week2/plan.md` written: Friday topic, 5 short posts, 3 watch-list changes, 1 experiment

- [ ] At least 5 replies sent today

## Looking ahead

Week 2 is when the engine actually starts producing. Week 1 was scaffolding — necessary but not the work itself. Mercury AD has roughly 11 weeks left after this. Make every Friday count.

If your Week 1 numbers feel disappointing, that's normal. Almost no one hits real traction in week 1. The week-on-week growth curve is what matters — small in absolute terms early, compounding through Mercury AD, breakouts in months 2–3 if the work is real.
