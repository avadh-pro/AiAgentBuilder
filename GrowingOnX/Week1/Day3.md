### 3. Decide the pinned-post topic (60 min)
Pinned post = your most-viewed asset for the next 6+ months. It must:
- Be **research-grade** (not a hot take)
- State your **positioning explicitly** in tweet 1 or 2
- Deliver **one specific piece of useful work** (a breakdown, a benchmark, a framework, a misconception corrected)
- Be **re-readable** — interesting enough that someone landing on it 6 months from now still reads to the end

Brainstorm 5 candidate topics in `Week1/pinned-post-options.md`. Pick one tonight and write a one-paragraph synopsis underneath it.

**Topic seeds to consider:**
- A breakdown of a specific agent paper through your "older systems thinking" lens
- A misconception you've encountered repeatedly about how production agents fail (reframe generically — no employer name)
- A taxonomy of agent failure modes you've personally hit and patched
- A first-principles essay on why current agent benchmarks measure the wrong thing
- An archaeological reading of a 1970s/80s systems-thinking paper (Beer, Ashby, Forrester, Simon, Vyas) applied to modern agent design

The chart is loud about which kind of pinned post wins for *you*: depth + investigation + non-Western frame. Avoid "10 prompts every founder needs" type content — it'll underperform compared to a single dense research piece.
